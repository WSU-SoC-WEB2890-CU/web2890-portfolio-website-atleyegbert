    Life Coach Website
    Prototype

    Project Description

    Client:

    Britta Blanchard, Life Coach

    Tools + Technology:

    Figma

    Role:

    UX Designer, UX Research

    Total Time To Complete:

    14 Weeks

    View Prototype

    Initially, potential clients interested in working with Britta were not able to find information about her services, get in contact with her, or register for events she hosts. My goal in creating this prototype was to create a design that would facilitate connections with potential clients through: Providing meaningful information about Britta’s personal and professional background, allowing users to contact her easily on her site and connect with her on other social media platforms, and creating an opportunity and pathway for users to register for hosted group events directly on her site.

    Design Process

    • Conduct User Interviews

      I conducted user interviews with women who were in the target demographic for this life coach. This opportunity provided me with crucial feedback about the needs, goals, motivations, and concerns my users would likely have. After conducting these interviews, I used my findings to develop a persona which I could use as a reference throughout the design process.

    • Develop User Flows, Determine Taxonomy + Sitemap

      I created user flows to model three tasks that my users would likely need to complete while visiting this website. These tasks were to fill out a contact form, explore information about Britta, and register for an event.
      I determined the taxonomy and sitemap for this website prototype by considering how users would likely navigate through the site to accomplish tasks and find important information. This step of the design process was crucial for me to decide how I would implement the main navigation.

    • Wireframes

      Creating wireframes for six pages featured in this prototype was the next step of my design process. I created the wireframes for a homepage, registration page, content page, search results page, about / contact page, and a 404 error page. I designed both the mobile and desktop versions of the pages. Doing this planning in advance made it much easier to create the final prototype later since I already had the basic outline of each page.

    • Creating a Style Guide

      While creating the style guide, it was really important to me that it would reflect the branding that this life coach has already established through her Instagram and that her personality would shine through. The color palette I chose incorporated the branding colors that this life coach already had and I made adjustments to in order to improve contrast. The fonts I chose for this site were similar to the ones she had already been using.
      This helped me while designing the final prototype to make sure I was being consistent with sizing across all of the pages. I also designed a logo and buttons, chose icons and images, and established words and phrases that reflected the overall feeling of the site. These words and phrases were “health and wellness”, “organic”, “luxurious”, and “powerful”.

    • Implementing the Prototype

      I used figma to implement my prototype design and allow users to interact with it. The style guide and persona I had previously designed really helped me throughout this step of the design process. I was able to reflect back on it for inspiration and to make sure I kept the overall feeling of the site the same as her branding.
      Creating the connections for users to interact with the prototype was pretty straightforward. My goal was for users to have the most realistic experience possible when testing this prototype. I wanted users to be able to have the experience of filling out the form so when they click on each of the input boxes it will autofill with information.

    • Conducting Usability Testing

      Conducting usability testing was the final step of my design process. I interviewed four users who would be a part of the target demographic for this website. I was able to receive a lot of helpful feedback from this testing which I used to make adjustments to my final design. From this testing, I learned that I need to make several changes to my site to make it easier to navigate and improve usability. This experience was really helpful for me to watch users interact with the prototype and hear their frustrations with the design as they were using it.
